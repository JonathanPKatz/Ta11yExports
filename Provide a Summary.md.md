<h2 id="summary">Summary</h2>
<p>In this information age, it often feels like there is too much to read. A good summary gives a reader enough information to quickly determine whether a web page or document meets their needs.</p>
<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>
<p>If you have ever used the acronyms BLUF (bottom line up front) or TL;DR (too long; didnâ€™t read), you already understand why a summary is important. A good summary helps readers quickly determine whether a web page or document has the content they want.</p>
<ul>
<li>The longer a document, the more critical the summary.</li>
<li>The more complex a document, the greater the need for a simple overview.</li>
</ul>
<p>A summary can save everyone time but especially benefits people with cognitive and learning disabilities.</p>
<h2 id="who-is-helped">Who is Helped</h2>
<p>People with disabilities affecting language, processing, or memory benefit from clear, easy-to-understand summaries. The summary highlights the purpose and content of the web page or document and helps these individuals decide if the content contains the information they need.</p>
<h2 id="guidelines">Guidelines</h2>
<p>Ensure summaries are easy to understand.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Provide a summary written at an 8th-grade (lower secondary education) reading level or lower.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>See <strong><a href="learning/topic?key=writing.w_reading_level">Check Reading Level (Ta11y)</a></strong> for more information.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Provide a summary at the beginning of a:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Document longer than a single page.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Long web page.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Link to long documents, long web pages, or a series of web pages about a single topic, such as a tutorial.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Headings can act as a summary for content with less than 300 words.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The goal of a summary is to provide enough information for the reader to decide whether the content meets their needs.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Describe key points using a few sentences or bullet points.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use keywords to help convey the purpose of the document.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use common words, short sentences, and plain language to make the summary easy to understand.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Follow the general guidance for writing a summary in articles like <a href="https://www.grammarly.com/blog/how-to-write-a-summary/">How to Write a Summary (Grammarly)</a>.</p>
